How to Get Rid of PMI

If you put less than 20% down when you bought your home, you're probably paying private mortgage insurance every month. PMI typically costs between $100 and $300 per month - money that goes straight to your lender's insurance, not your equity.

The good news: once your home's value has increased enough that you have 20% equity, most lenders will cancel PMI with a current appraisal. With Twin Cities home values having risen significantly in recent years, many homeowners qualify sooner than they think.

A PMI removal appraisal helps you:

Meet your lender's requirements - A certified appraisal is what most lenders need to process cancellation.

Eliminate monthly PMI payments - Stop paying insurance that no longer applies to your situation.

Save hundreds or thousands every year - That money stays in your pocket instead of your lender's.

Before You Start

Call your lender first and ask about their PMI cancellation process - some have specific requirements for which appraisers you can use or forms that need to be completed. Once you know the rules, we handle the rest: schedule the inspection, deliver the report, and provide documentation your lender will accept.

Important note: if your mortgage is federally backed (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac), your lender may be required to order the appraisal themselves through an AMC. If that's your situation, we can still help you understand your options. For state-chartered lenders and portfolio loans, you typically have more flexibility in choosing your appraiser.
