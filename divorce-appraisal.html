Why an Accurate Appraisal Matters in a Divorce

When you're dividing property in a divorce, the home is usually the biggest asset on the table. An inaccurate valuation - or skipping the appraisal entirely - can cost you thousands and fuel unnecessary conflict.

A professional appraisal gives both parties an impartial, defensible number to work from. It takes the guesswork and emotion out of the equation so you can focus on reaching a fair settlement.

A court-ready divorce appraisal helps you:

Avoid unnecessary disputes - An independent third-party valuation removes opinion from the process.

Ensure a fair property division - Both parties can rely on the same credible number.

Move forward with confidence - A defensible report stands up in mediation or court.

What to Expect

We schedule the on-site inspection at a time that works for you - the visit takes 30 to 60 minutes. You'll receive a thorough, court-ready report typically within a few days. The report is clear, well-documented, and written in plain language that both parties and their attorneys can understand.
